Country Detail Sheets

Germany

MEIKO Maschinenbau GmbH & Co. KG and local sales entities

Thresholds

  • Master File required when local sales exceeded EUR 100M in the prior financial year.
  • Local File triggered when goods RPTs exceed EUR 6M or other RPTs exceed EUR 600K.

Documentation Approach

  • Maintain BEPS Action 13 compliant MF/LF with refreshed benchmarking.
  • Document extraordinary transactions within six months of year end.

Forms and Disclosures

  • Transaction Matrix submitted automatically within 30 days of an audit notice.
  • Master File and extraordinary documentation accompany the Transaction Matrix.
  • CIT return disclosure lists foreign related parties and low-tax transactions.
Filing status: Transaction Matrix is a separate filing; CIT disclosure included with the return.

Deadlines

  • Maintain contemporaneous documentation throughout FY 2025.
  • Extraordinary transaction documentation due 30 Jun 2026.
  • Provide the Local File within 30 days of a formal request.

Austria

MEIKO Austria GmbH

Thresholds

  • MF/LF mandatory if local GAAP turnover exceeded EUR 50M in each of the two preceding financial years.
  • Scaled documentation expected even when thresholds are not met.

Documentation Approach

  • Maintain OECD-standard MF/LF with detailed functional coverage.
  • Address intercompany services and financing arrangements specifically.

Forms and Disclosures

  • CbCR notification due 31 Dec 2025.
Filing status: CbCR notification filed separately; MF/LF retained for audit.

Deadlines

  • MF/LF recommended by 31 Mar 2026.
  • CbCR notification due 31 Dec 2025.

Belgium

MEIKO Clean Solutions Belgium SRL

Thresholds

  • MF/LF required when revenue >= EUR 50M, assets >= EUR 1B, or FTE >= 100.
  • Local File Part II required if cross-border RPTs exceed EUR 1M.

Documentation Approach

  • Submit OECD-aligned MF/LF with enhanced value chain and DEMPE detail.
  • Attach TP policies, agreements, APAs, and insurance contracts when filing the Local File.

Forms and Disclosures

  • Form 275.MF (Master File).
  • Form 275.LF (Local File).
  • Form 275.CBC.NOT (CbCR notification).
Filing status: Form 275.LF filed with the CIT return; other forms submitted separately.

Deadlines

  • Form 275.CBC.NOT due 31 Dec 2025.
  • Form 275.LF filed with the CIT return (expected 31 Jul 2026).
  • Form 275.MF due 31 Dec 2026.

Deadlines

Critical Action Items

Immediate Priorities

  • Germany: Enable 30-day delivery of the Transaction Matrix, Master File, and extraordinary documentation upon audit notice.
  • Belgium: Update Form 275 packages with enhanced disclosures and ensure supporting evidence is ready for e-filing.
  • France: Adjust documentation to the EUR 150M threshold and account for higher penalties.
  • Netherlands: Build workflows for Public CbCR preparation, review, and publication.

2025 Regulatory Changes

  • Germany: Automatic submission of the Transaction Matrix and Master File; Local File response window reduced to 30 days.
  • Belgium: Revised Form 275 series with expanded value chain and DEMPE coverage.
  • France: MF/LF threshold lowered to EUR 150M with increased penalties.
  • Netherlands: EU Public CbCR effective for FY 2025 (publication by 31 Dec 2026).
  • Denmark: Threshold raised with a de minimis exemption (monitor for future applicability).

Process Improvements

  • Centralise MF/LF templates and country annexes for consistent annual updates.
  • Track whether each TP form is filed with or separate from the CIT return and assign accountable owners.
  • Align statutory accounts, tax returns, and TP narratives to avoid inconsistencies (especially for Public CbCR).
  • Strengthen rapid-response protocols for short timelines (Germany, Spain, Japan).

Data and Governance

  • Refresh intercompany agreements and supporting documentation (Belgium, Turkey, Mexico).
  • Confirm revenue benchmarks for MF/LF thresholds annually (France, Austria, Netherlands, Turkey, UAE).
  • Segment filings across Tax, Finance, and Legal teams with clear ownership and tracking.
  • Provide quarterly board reports summarising compliance status and open risks.

Decision Framework

  2. Map semi-finished and finished goods flows between manufacturing hubs and markets.
  3. Review local statutory requirements before finalising the TP methodology.
