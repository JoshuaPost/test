Country Detail Sheets

Germany

Meiko Maschinenbau GmbH & Co. KG and local sales entities

Thresholds

  • Master File required if German entity sales exceed EUR 100 million in the preceding FY.
  • Local File required if goods RPTs > EUR 6 million OR other RPTs > EUR 600,000.

Documentation Approach

  • Maintain BEPS Action 13 compliant MF/LF with refreshed benchmarking.
  • Document extraordinary transactions within six months of year end.

Forms and Disclosures

  • Transaction Matrix (NEW): Required structured overview of RPTs.
  • Master File, Transaction Matrix, and Extraordinary RPT Documentation must be submitted automatically upon audit announcement.
Filing status: Automatic submission within 30 days of tax audit order; LF submitted upon request.

Deadlines

  • Submission (Automatic): Within 30 days of receiving the tax audit order.
  • LF Submission (Upon Request): Within 30 days.

Austria

Meiko Austria GmbH

Thresholds

  • MF/LF mandatory if local GAAP turnover exceeded EUR 50M in each of the two preceding financial years.
  • Scaled documentation expected even when thresholds are not met.

Documentation Approach

  • Maintain OECD-standard MF/LF with detailed functional coverage.
  • Address intercompany services and financing arrangements specifically.

Forms and Disclosures

  • CbCR notification due 31 Dec 2025.
Filing status: CbCR notification filed separately; MF/LF retained for audit.

Deadlines

  • CbCR notification due 31 Dec 2025.
  • MF/LF submission: Upon request, within 30 days after filing the tax return.

Belgium

Meiko Clean Solutions Belgium SRL

Thresholds

  • MF/LF required when revenue >= EUR 50M, assets >= EUR 1B, or FTE >= 100.
  • Local File Part II required if cross-border RPTs exceed EUR 1M.

Documentation Approach

  • Submit OECD-aligned MF/LF with enhanced value chain and DEMPE detail.
  • Attach TP policies, agreements, APAs, and insurance contracts when filing the Local File.

Forms and Disclosures

  • Form 275.MF (Master File submission form).
  • Form 275.LF (Local File submission form).
  • Form 275.CBC.NOT (CbCR Notification, required only if information changes from prior year).
Filing status: Form 275.LF filed with the CIT return; other forms submitted separately.

Deadlines

  • Form 275.CBC.NOT due 31 Dec 2025.
  • Form 275.LF filed with the CIT return (expected 31 Jul 2026).
  • Form 275.MF due 31 Dec 2026.

France

Meiko FRANCE S.A.S

Thresholds

  • MF/LF required (NEW) when group turnover or gross assets ≥ EUR 150 million (for FYs starting 01/01/2024).

  • Account for higher minimum penalties for inadequate documentation.

Filing status: CERFA 2257-SD filed separately within six months after the legal deadline for the CIT return.

Deadlines

  • CERFA 2257-SD submission: Due at latest within six months after the legal deadline for the CIT return (approx. 31 Oct 2026).
  • MF/LF submission: Upon request, due within 30 days.

Turkey

Meiko Turkey

Thresholds

  • Master File not mandatory (Group revenue < EUR 750M).
  • Local File mandatory if local entity meets specific size thresholds: Assets ≥ TRY 500 million (≈ EUR 15 million) AND Turnover ≥ TRY 500 million (≈ EUR 15 million).

Documentation Approach

  • Follow Annex I and II of the Turkish TP Guidelines.
  • Cover domestic and cross-border related party transactions.

Forms and Disclosures

  • TP Return/Form: A separate TP return must be filed as an appendix to the CIT return.
Filing status: TP return submitted with the CIT return; MF/LF retained separately.

Deadlines

  • TP Return submission: No later than the CIT return deadline (31 Mar 2026).
  • MF/LF preparation: 12 months after FYE (31 Dec 2026).

Malaysia

Functional Profiles and TP Scenarios

Scenario Primary IP Owner(s) Manufacturing Role Sales / Distribution Implications
Current Assessment Germany most likely the entrepreneurial manufacturer and global IP owner. US and China facilities operate as contract, licensed, or regional IP manufacturers depending on final model. Scenario modelling needed to confirm profit allocation; supports either royalty or cost-plus flows.
Scenario 1 – Central IP Germany retains all IP rights. US and China act as contract manufacturers earning 5–10% TNMM markup; Germany supplies semi-finished goods on cost-plus. Germany licenses distributors (3–7% royalties); straightforward hub-and-spoke pricing.
Scenario 2 – Shared IP Germany, US, and China share IP ownership tied to DEMPE activities. Each manufacturer retains entrepreneurial exposure; profit split applied across the network. No internal royalties; semi-finished goods valued via contribution-based allocation.
Scenario 3 – Regional IP Each manufacturing hub owns its regional rights and cross-licenses for exports. Facilities assume regional entrepreneur roles with local market risk. Inter-regional transfers priced using market benchmarks; increased focus on DEMPE and license governance.

Decision Framework

  1. Confirm legal and economic IP ownership across the group.
  2. Map semi-finished and finished goods flows between manufacturing hubs and markets.
  3. Review local statutory requirements before finalising the TP methodology.

Critical Information Needed

  • Definitive IP ownership structure and history of development.
  • Detailed intercompany product flow data (volumes, values, routes).
  • Comprehensive functional analysis across functions, assets, and risks.
  • Inventory of legal agreements, APAs, and historic profit allocation outcomes.
